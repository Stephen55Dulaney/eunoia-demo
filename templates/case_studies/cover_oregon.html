Cover Oregon UX Case Study

Experience Architecture & Usability Lead: Stephen Dulaney

  • Role: UX Lead, Experience Architecture & Usability
  • Engagement Period: July 2012 – May 2013
  • Focus: Enrollment workflow design & extensive usability testing

Context & Project Background

Cover Oregon (Oct 1, 2012 – Mar 6, 2015) was Oregon's ACA health insurance exchange. Stephen Dulaney served as UX Lead for Experience Architecture & Usability from July 2012 to May 2013, directing a team of ~5 UX practitioners to establish foundational UX artifacts and prototypes.

Stephen's Responsibilities:

  • Led discovery workshops and JAD sessions with state stakeholders, Deloitte leadership, and vendor teams.
  • Developed a high-fidelity HTML5/CSS/jQuery prototype simulating core enrollment processes.
  • Conducted 60+ in-person usability testing sessions with diverse participants, including multilingual support.
  • Integrated multi-language considerations (e.g., Spanish) in prototype and design.
  • Defined and oversaw accessibility requirements and audit remediation guidance.
  • Collaborated with development/QA to integrate interaction specs, test scripts, and analytics instrumentation.

Business Problem: Enable reliable online enrollment for Oregonians; early prototypes revealed key usability and technical challenges (identity verification, connectivity, complexity).

Success Metrics:

  • Online enrollment completion target (baseline low due to early failures).
  • Time-to-complete <30 minutes.
  • Error/fallback rate minimized with clear recovery guidance.
  • User satisfaction ≥4/5 in testing.
  • Manual processing reduction via improved online success.
  • WCAG compliance and multilingual support.

Research & User Testing

Stephen's Approach:

  • Stakeholder Workshops: Requirements and alignment sessions with Oregon Health Authority IT, vendor teams, and call-center managers.
  • Persona & Journey Mapping: Created and refined personas (low-income applicants, rural users, small business owners, assisters) and mapped enrollment journeys.
  • Usability Testing: 60+ in-person sessions covering identity verification, plan selection, submission, error/fallback, accessibility, and low-bandwidth scenarios.
  • Heuristic & Accessibility Audit: Applied Nielsen heuristics and WCAG 2.1 AA checklists to prototypes and existing screens; provided remediation guidance.
  • Support Log & Analytics Collaboration: Reviewed enrollment error logs and call-center data to prioritize fixes and test scenarios.
  • Comparative Benchmarking: Studied HealthCare.gov and peer exchanges for best practices in progress indicators, inline validation, and feedback.

Key Personas

Maria, The Budget-Conscious Seeker

Background: Low-income applicant; limited digital literacy; anxious about deadlines.

Goals: Complete enrollment with clear guidance and save/resume ability.

Pain Points: Verification failures; confusing instructions; language barriers.

"I tried online but kept hitting errors; I worried I missed the deadline."

David, The Employee-Focused Entrepreneur

Background: Small business owner; moderate tech comfort; limited time.

Goals: Efficient plan comparison; bulk employee enrollment support.

Pain Points: Overwhelming comparison tables; no multi-client interface.

"I want a quick overview of options and to know if my employees are enrolled."

Eleanor, The Remote Navigator

Background: Rural resident; intermittent connectivity; mobile or public access.

Goals: Continue enrollment despite network issues.

Pain Points: Lost progress, heavy pages, unclear save options.

"I lost progress when my connection dropped; then I gave up."

Robert, The Community Advocate

Background: Healthcare assister/broker; high digital proficiency; supports many clients.

Goals: Dashboard for monitoring client statuses; quick error resolution.

Pain Points: Manual spreadsheets; inconsistent error reporting.

"I need a dashboard to see which clients are stuck and why."

Insights & Pain Points

  • Identity Verification Complexity: Cryptic failures; need inline guidance.
  • Multi-Step Form Usability: Loss of context; lack of save/resume; timeouts.
  • Error Messaging & Recovery: No actionable next steps; users unsure how to retry or get help.
  • Plan Comparison Overload: Dense tables; lack of visual summaries and calculators.
  • Low-Bandwidth & Offline Risks: Slow load times; absence of offline guidance/autosave.
  • No Assister Dashboard: Brokers used spreadsheets; inefficiency and errors.
  • Accessibility Barriers: Missing labels, low contrast, keyboard traps; multilingual support needed.
  • Trust & Feedback Gaps: Users needed clear status updates to reduce anxiety.

Design & Prototype

Ideation & Key Concepts

  • Visual Plan Comparison Dashboard: Filters, visual cards, subsidy calculator.
  • Contextual Help & Agent Connect: Persistent help icons linking to tips or live assistance.
  • Multi-Step Save-&-Resume Form: Progress bar, inline tooltips, autosave triggers.
  • Assister Dashboard: Client table with statuses and quick actions.
  • Error Card Component: Clear messaging, unique codes, actionable buttons.
  • Notification Center: Opt-in notifications at key milestones.
  • Accessibility Mode & Multilingual Support: High contrast, scalable text, ARIA attributes, language selector.
  • Offline Guidance Overlay: Autosave prompts and printable summary on network drop.

Prioritization

  • Quick Wins: Error handling, contextual help, accessibility fixes, notifications.
  • Strategic: Identity verification overhaul, offline save/resume.
  • Medium: Assister Dashboard, plan comparison enhancements.
  • Low Effort/Medium Impact: UI refinements, inline guidance.

Prototype Development

Tech Stack: HTML5, semantic markup; CSS responsive layouts; jQuery interactivity.

Testing & Iteration: 60+ usability sessions covering identity verification, submission, offline scenarios, accessibility. Iterated labels, feedback timing, and performance optimizations based on metrics (task success >80%, time-on-task <25 min, confidence ratings).

Accessibility & Multilingual: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance: semantic HTML, ARIA attributes, keyboard navigation, high contrast, scalable text, language selector (e.g., Spanish).

Outcomes & Impact

Business Impact

  • Increased online enrollment completion toward target ≥70%.
  • ~50% reduction in call-center inquiries due to clearer guidance and error messaging.
  • Improved operational efficiency via autosave and triage features.
  • Reduced manual processing costs by enabling more users to complete online flows.
  • Enhanced stakeholder confidence through validated prototypes and early testing.

User Experience Impact

  • Task success rates >80% in identity verification and submission flows.
  • Average completion times <25 minutes in testing scenarios.
  • Higher user confidence and satisfaction due to transparent feedback and notifications.
  • Inclusive access: offline resume capability confirmed; accessibility tests passed WCAG benchmarks.
  • Assister efficiency: ~30% time savings in tracking multiple client enrollments.

Learnings & Future Directions

  • Embed UX Lead early in project lifecycle for high-stakes government platforms.
  • Pilot incremental releases to validate assumptions before full launch.
  • Collaborate closely with vendor teams (Oracle integrators) on UX requirements and performance benchmarks.
  • Prioritize autosave/offline support for low-bandwidth users to reduce abandonment.
  • Integrate accessibility and multilingual support from the start; include testing in all sessions.
  • Define standardized error codes/messages early; integrate with support workflows.
  • Incorporate contextual help and transparent status updates to build trust.
  • Continuously monitor analytics to detect drop-offs and run rapid UX iterations.
  • Apply cross-sector best practices (enterprise self-service, CMS redesigns) to public-sector platforms.
  • Use lightweight prototypes (HTML/CSS/jQuery) for fast validation and iteration.
