Cover Oregon UX Case Study

A deep dive into the experience architecture and usability leadership for Oregon's Affordable Care Act health insurance exchange.

My Role

UX Lead, Experience Architecture & Usability

Engagement Period

July 2012 – May 2013

Core Focus

Enrollment workflow design & extensive usability testing

Context & Project Background

Cover Oregon (active from Oct 1, 2012 – Mar 6, 2015) was the State of Oregon's ambitious implementation of the Affordable Care Act (ACA) health insurance exchange. As the UX Lead for Experience Architecture & Usability, Stephen Dulaney directed a team of approximately five UX practitioners. The primary objective was to establish foundational UX artifacts, including high-fidelity prototypes, to guide the development of a complex, multi-faceted public system.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Led discovery workshops and Joint Application Design (JAD) sessions with state stakeholders, Deloitte leadership, and various vendor teams to align on goals and requirements.
  • Developed and maintained a high-fidelity HTML5/CSS/jQuery prototype that simulated the core enrollment processes, serving as a dynamic blueprint for development.
  • Conducted over 60 in-person usability testing sessions with a diverse range of Oregonians, including providing multilingual support to ensure broad accessibility.
  • Championed and integrated multi-language considerations (e.g., Spanish) directly into the prototype and overall design strategy.
  • Defined accessibility requirements based on WCAG standards and provided audit remediation guidance to ensure compliance.
  • Collaborated closely with development and QA teams to integrate interaction specifications, write usability-focused test scripts, and define analytics instrumentation for tracking user behavior.

The Business Problem

The core challenge was to enable reliable and accessible online health insurance enrollment for all Oregonians. Early prototypes and system iterations had revealed significant usability and technical hurdles, including complex identity verification processes, inconsistent connectivity for rural users, and overwhelming application complexity.

Success Metrics

  • Achieve a high online enrollment completion target, moving users from a failing paper-based system.
  • Reduce the average time-to-complete an application to under 30 minutes.
  • Minimize error and fallback rates by providing clear, contextual recovery guidance.
  • Attain a user satisfaction score of 4 out of 5 or higher in usability testing.
  • Decrease manual processing costs by improving the success rate of online applications.
  • Ensure full WCAG compliance and provide robust multilingual support.

Research & User Testing

Stephen's Research Approach:

  • Stakeholder Workshops: Conducted intensive requirements and alignment sessions with key players from the Oregon Health Authority, various IT vendor teams, and call-center managers to gather diverse perspectives.
  • Persona & Journey Mapping: Created and continuously refined a set of user personas representing Oregon's diverse population (e.g., low-income applicants, rural users, small business owners, and enrollment assisters) and mapped their end-to-end enrollment journeys.
  • In-depth Usability Testing: Executed over 60 in-person testing sessions that covered critical user flows, including identity verification, plan selection, application submission, error handling, and accessibility scenarios.
  • Heuristic & Accessibility Audits: Applied Nielsen's heuristics and WCAG 2.1 AA checklists to all prototypes and existing screens, providing actionable remediation guidance to development teams.
  • Data-Driven Insights: Reviewed early enrollment error logs and call-center data to identify common pain points, which directly informed test scenarios and prioritization of fixes.
  • Comparative Benchmarking: Studied HealthCare.gov and other state exchanges to identify and adapt best practices for progress indicators, inline validation, and user feedback mechanisms.

Key Personas

Maria, The Budget-Conscious Seeker

Low-income applicant; limited digital literacy; anxious about deadlines.

"I tried online but kept hitting errors; I worried I missed the deadline."

David, The Employee-Focused Entrepreneur

Small business owner; moderate tech comfort; limited time.

"I want a quick overview of options and to know if my employees are enrolled."

Susan, The Assister

Community health worker; needs multi-client support; requires clear admin tools.

"I need a dashboard to track my clients' progress and help them remotely."

James, The Rural User

Farmer with unreliable internet; values save/resume; needs clear offline options.

"My internet cuts out. I need to be able to save my application and come back later."

Insights & Pain Points

  • Identity Verification Complexity: Users faced cryptic failures during identity verification, highlighting a need for inline guidance and clearer validation messages.
  • Multi-Step Form Usability: The lengthy, multi-step forms led to a loss of context, user fatigue, and high abandonment rates, exacerbated by a lack of save/resume functionality and session timeouts.
  • Error Messaging & Recovery: When errors occurred, the system provided no actionable next steps, leaving users unsure how to retry, get help, or correct their information.
  • Plan Comparison Overload: The interface for comparing health plans consisted of dense data tables that overwhelmed users, making it difficult to make informed decisions.
  • Low-Bandwidth & Offline Risks: Rural and mobile users struggled with slow load times and lost their progress when connectivity dropped, as there was no offline guidance or autosave feature.
  • No Assister Dashboard: Brokers and healthcare assisters were forced to use manual spreadsheets to track clients, leading to inefficiency and data entry errors.

Design & Prototype

Key Design Concepts

  • Visual Plan Comparison Dashboard: A redesigned dashboard with filters, visual summary cards, and an integrated subsidy calculator to simplify plan selection.
  • Contextual Help & Agent Connect: Persistent help icons that linked to contextual tips, FAQs, or an option to connect with a live agent.
  • Multi-Step Save-&-Resume Form: A chunked form with a clear progress bar, inline validation, tooltips, and autosave triggers to prevent data loss.
  • Assister Dashboard: A dedicated portal for healthcare assisters featuring a client table with statuses, alerts, and quick actions to manage multiple applications efficiently.
  • Standardized Error Card Component: A friendly, consistent error component with clear messaging, unique error codes for support, and actionable buttons.

Prototype Development

A high-fidelity prototype was built using HTML5, CSS, and jQuery to simulate the core user flows. This allowed for rapid testing and iteration before costly development began.

Business Impact

  • Increased online enrollment completion toward target of ≥70%.
  • Projected 50% reduction in call-center inquiries due to clearer guidance.
  • Improved operational efficiency via autosave and application triage features.
  • Enhanced stakeholder confidence through validated prototypes and early user testing.

User Experience Impact

  • Task success rates exceeded 80% in key flows like identity verification.
  • Average application completion times were reduced to under 25 minutes in testing.
  • Higher user confidence and satisfaction ratings due to transparent feedback.
  • Inclusive access was confirmed through successful offline/resume and accessibility tests.
