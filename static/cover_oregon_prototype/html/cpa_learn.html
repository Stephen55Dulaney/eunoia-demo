Agents and community partners

Agents

Cover Oregon is partnering with insurance agents who can help us offer better access to health coverage for all Oregonians. More than 600,000 currently uninsured Oregonians will have new opportunities to shop for health coverage this fall. Becoming an affiliated Cover Oregon insurance agent can give you a new tool for assisting your current clients and an opportunity to expand your book of business. Affiliated agents with Cover Oregon can sell all plans offered through Cover Oregon regardless of existing carrier appointments. Only agents with an Oregon health license can become affiliated with Cover Oregon.

To become a Cover Oregon affiliated agent, you will need to take part in a two-part training program. Training sessions are currently underway. Click here to enroll in training.

Community partners

Cover Oregon is partnering with the Oregon Health Authority (OHA) on an outreach program for hard-to-reach, non-English speaking, geographically isolated, and underserved populations. As part of this program, Cover Oregon and OHA will work with community partner organizations to provide local, on-the-ground outreach and application assistance for individuals and families.

Community partner organizations may have staff and/or volunteers trained to conduct application assistance. Required training for potential assisters includes an online prerequisite followed by an in-person training session. Click here to enroll in training. Additionally, partner organizations must enter into a formal agreement with OHA as a community outreach and enrollment grantee, provider enrollment grantee or as a volunteer organization.

Volunteer opportunities

Community partners interested in helping individuals and families apply for health insurance through Cover Oregon’s online marketplace can submit an application in response to the request for application and contract with OHA on a volunteer basis. The RFA is now posted on ORPIN, and will also be available on www.coveroregon.com. The RFA is open through November 2014 and may be extended.

Closed grant opportunities

  • Outreach & Enrollment Grant: In April 2013, OHA released a RFGP seeking community partners to work within their communities and help hard-to-reach populations enroll in health coverage, including non-English speaking and geographically isolated communities. This first round of grant applications is now closed.
  • Small Business: In August 2013, Cover Oregon released a RFGP seeking business associations to outreach to their business members on behalf of Cover Oregon. The grant application is closed and grant-funded outreach activities to the business community is expected to begin in October 2013.
  • Tribal: Cover Oregon provided Oregon’s nine federally recognized Tribes with funds to provide outreach and enrollment in their communities. Most of Oregon’s Tribal communities are located in geographically isolated areas and often shy away from outsiders. Tribal Communities Partners will be insiders and have experience with outreach and education for health programs in their communities. Funding is temporary starting September 1, 2013 and ending April 30, 2013.
