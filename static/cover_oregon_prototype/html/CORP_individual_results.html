3 ways to help you pay for coverage

Individuals and families will find out if they are eligible for premium tax credits or public programs through a simple application process on the Cover Oregon website.. To qualify for assistance, you must be an Oregon resident and U.S. citizen or legal immigrant. The type and amount of financial assistance you receive is based on a sliding scale determined by annual before-tax income, family size, age. These types of financial assistance include:

  • Premium Tax Credits
  • Medicaid (Oregon Health Plan)
  • Healthy Kids (CHIP)

Use our financial assistance calculator to find out if you might qualify, and if so, an estimate of the assistance you may expect. Please keep in mind these numbers are a preliminary estimate.

Premium Tax Credits

Premium tax credits help offset the monthly costs of health insurance premiums. The amount you will receive is based on a sliding scale determined by household income and family size. See the illustrations to see estimated maximum monthly payments for an individual and family of 4. Tax credits can be refundable and/or advanceable. A refundable tax credit is available to an individual even if he or she has no tax liability. An advanceable tax credit allows an individual to receive assistance at the time of purchase rather than paying their premium out of pocket and waiting to be reimbursed when filing their annual tax returns.

Medicaid (Oregon Health Plan)

Healthy Kids is a public program for uninsured Oregon children and teens under 19 years old. Healthy Kids includes medical, dental, vision, prescription drugs, mental health coverage, and substance abuse treatment. Even middle-income families can qualify for no-cost or low-cost coverage. Once you are enrolled, a child will usually receive coverage for an entire year – and can potentially renew this coverage every year.

CHIP (Healthy Kids)

Medicaid provides coverage for people who may not be able to cover the high costs of health care. The Oregon Health Plan (OHP) is a state health care coverage program for uninsured adults with low incomes. Once a person is enrolled, he or she will receive coverage for one full year. In most cases, adults must have been without medical insurance for 6 months to qualify for OHP. There are exceptions. For example, pregnant women may have other medical insurance and still qualify.
