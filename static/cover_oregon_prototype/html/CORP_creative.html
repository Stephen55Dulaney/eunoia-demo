Long Live Oregonians!

Cover Oregon's statewide outreach and marketing campaign aims to reach as many Oregonians as possible to let them know about the options available to them through Cover Oregon, the state of Oregon’s new online health insurance marketplace.

Our ad campaign, "Long Live Oregonians" features TV, radio and online ads across the state. Also underway is a high impact, on-the-ground outreach effort in partnership with agents, community organizations, Tribes, providers, business associations, statewide agencies and other key partners across the state.

About the Ads

To create ads that are truly by Oregonians and for Oregonians, Cover Oregon called upon local musicians and artists. We were inspired by the song Bonneville Power Administration asked Woody Guthrie to write to help explain how the Bonneville Dam would benefit Oregonians. We decided to only work with local artists--in that way, were able to put dollars back into the Oregon artist community and into a population of people who are traditionally uninsured.

The first TV ad features the Cover Oregon anthem, “Long Live Oregonians,” featuring Portland-based musician Matt Sheehy of the band Lost Lander, along with an ode to Cover Oregon, “Live Long in Oregon” from Laura Gibson, a Coquille, Oregon native who is currently touring with the Portland Cello Project. The campaign also includes radio ads from Lifesavas, Vikesh Kapoor, Willie Vlautin and Dave Depper as well as artwork for online and out-of-home ads from artists across the state.

Radio Ads

  • "We Fly With Our Own Wings" is sung by Dave Depper, a musician in Portland who also composes music for movies, television and advertisements.
  • "Live Long in Oregon" from Laura Gibson, raised in Coquille, Oregon, who is currently touring with the Portland Cello Project.
  • "To the Future" features Lifesavas, the Portland-based hip hop duo of MC Vursatyl and producer/MC Jumbo the Garbageman.
  • "We Feel Free" is a song featuring Vikesh Kapoor, a Portland musician who was inspired by the battle against class/race injustice.
  • "Health Care for All” is sung by author and singer Willie Vlautin of the Portland band Richmond Fontaine.

Online Ads

We asked visual artists of all forms (illustrators, painters, letter-press, etc.) to help us express the Cover Oregon mission. Not only did this empower Oregonians to share their view of Cover Oregon and help us create communications that feel true to the communities across the state, it allowed us to put the Cover Oregon marketing dollars right back into the state, and into a population that is historically uninsured. This artwork will be used throughout our digital and Out-Of-Home (billboards, bus shelters) ads. Check out some of our artwork below.

Also, be sure to take a look at the presentation on Cover Oregon's advertising campaign, "Long Live Oregonians," given to the media on Monday July 8 featuring Chief Communications Officer Amy Fauver. You can listen to the audio version of the presentation here.

Even if you miss our continued ads, stay informed with newsletters about health coverage options available through Cover Oregon beginning in October 2013.
