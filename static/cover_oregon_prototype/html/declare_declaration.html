Submit your household information

I agree to these terms and conditions under penalty of perjury, which means I’ve provided true answers to all the questions to the best of my knowledge. I know that I may be subject to penalties under federal law if I provide false or untrue information.

I know that I must tell the Oregon Health Authority (OHA) or Cover Oregon if anything changes (and is different than) what I provided on this application. I can visit www.healthcare.gov or call 1-855-CoverOR (1-855-268-3767) to report any changes. I understand that a change in information could affect the qualification of member(s) of my household.

I know that under federal law, discrimination isn’t permitted on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, age, sexual orientation, gender identity or disability. I can file for a complaint of discrimination by visiting www.hhs.gov/ocr/office/file.

I recognize that Cover Oregon needs this information to check qualification for help paying for health coverage if I choose to apply. Cover Oregon will need to check the answers I’ve provided using information in its electronic databases, as well as in databases from the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), Social Security, the Department of Homeland Security, a consumer reporting agency and other state and federal sources. If the information doesn’t match, I may be required to submit the appropriate documentation as proof.

If anyone in my household is applying for qualification for the Oregon Health Plan (OHP)/Healthy Kids, I give OHA the right to pursue and get any money from other health insurance, legal settlements or other third parties. I also give OHA the right to pursue and get medical support from a spouse or parent. If any child in my household has a parent living outside of the home, I know I will be asked to cooperate with the agency that collects medical support from an absent parent. If I think that cooperating to collect medical support will harm me or my children, I can tell OHA and I may not have to cooperate.

If the person receiving health benefits dies, the state may recover the amount of medical benefits received after the age of 55 from the estate of the person who received benefits. This includes monthly payments made by OHA to managed care plans. In cases where the person receiving benefits is in an institution (such as a nursing home) for six months prior to death, the state will recover money for all medical benefits provided regardless of age when received. The state will not claim this money if the person receiving benefits has children who are under the age of 21, blind or permanently and totally disabled. If the person receiving benefits has a spouse, the state will wait until the spouse dies before claiming money.

If I think OHA or Cover Oregon has made a mistake, I can request a review and appeal the decision. To request a review means to tell someone at OHA or Cover Oregon that I think the action is wrong and ask for a fair review of the action. I know I can find out how to appeal by contacting 1-855-CoverOR (1-855-268-3767). I know I can choose to have someone represent me during the review process. My qualification and other important information will be explained to me.

Cover Oregon reserves the right to update and modify these terms of service at any time, at its sole discretion and without prior notice. Modified terms of service will be effective immediately upon posting. By continuing my use of the Cover Oregon website, I hereby agree to the modified terms of service. By clicking “I have read and agreed to the terms of service,” I also agree to review the terms of service periodically. My continued use following such modifications will indicate my acceptance of the new terms of service. If I do not agree with the modified terms of service, my only remedy is to discontinue using the Cover Oregon website.
